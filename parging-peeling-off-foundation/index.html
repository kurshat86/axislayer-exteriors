Why Foundation Parging Peels or Falls Off

When parging starts peeling away from your foundation, it usually means the bond between the coating and concrete has failed. This is often caused by moisture intrusion, poor adhesion, or long-term weather exposure in Edmonton’s freeze-thaw climate.

What Causes Parging to Peel Off?

Parging typically peels when the bond between the coating and the concrete fails. This can happen due to trapped moisture behind the surface, improper surface preparation, or natural aging of the material.

In Edmonton, repeated freeze-thaw cycles make this worse by expanding moisture trapped behind the surface, forcing the parging to separate.

Signs of Parging Delamination

  • Sections of parging lifting or curling away
  • Chunks of material falling off the wall
  • Exposed concrete or block underneath
  • Dark moisture staining behind failed areas

Why This Is More Serious Than Cracking

Unlike surface cracks, peeling indicates a deeper failure between the coating and the foundation. This allows moisture to move freely behind the surface, accelerating deterioration.

Can You Patch Peeling Parging?

Small localized repairs may be possible, but in most cases, peeling parging needs to be properly removed and reapplied to ensure long-term adhesion.

How Professional Repair Works

Repair typically involves removing all loose material, cleaning and preparing the foundation surface, and applying a new bonded parging system designed to withstand moisture and freeze-thaw conditions. Our parging repair process ensures the new layer bonds permanently to your concrete.

If your foundation is experiencing surface failure, professional foundation parging in Edmonton can restore protection and prevent further damage.

Why This Happens in Edmonton

Edmonton’s climate creates constant expansion and contraction cycles in exterior materials. When moisture gets behind parging, freezing conditions force it to expand, breaking the bond between the surface and the concrete.
