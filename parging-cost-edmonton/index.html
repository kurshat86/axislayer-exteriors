How Much Does Foundation Parging Cost in Edmonton?

The cost of foundation parging in Edmonton depends on surface condition, size of the area, and finish type. Most residential projects fall within a few key price ranges depending on whether it’s a repair, resurfacing, or full application. If you aren't sure if your home actually requires a new coat yet, our parging necessity guide can help you evaluate your foundation's current state.

Typical Parging Cost in Edmonton

Most foundation parging projects in Edmonton range depending on condition and scope:

  • Small repairs: $500 – $1,500
  • Partial resurfacing: $1,500 – $4,000
  • Full foundation parging: $4,000 – $10,000+

What Affects the Cost?

  • Size of foundation surface
  • Existing damage ( cracks, peeling, moisture issues)
  • Surface preparation required
  • Access around the home
  • Type of finish selected

Acrylic vs Textured Parging Finishes

The finish type is one of the biggest cost differences in modern parging work.

Acrylic Finish (Premium)

  • Smoother, more modern appearance
  • Higher flexibility and crack resistance
  • Better moisture resistance
  • Typical cost: higher end of range

Traditional Textured Cement Finish

  • Rough, classic concrete look
  • More affordable option
  • Good durability, but less flexible
  • Typical cost: lower to mid range

Repair vs Full Replacement Cost

Small repairs (like patching cracks or peeling sections) are significantly cheaper than full resurfacing. However, if moisture damage or widespread failure exists, choosing a professional parging repair service for a full removal and reapplication is often more cost-effective long-term.

Is Parging Worth the Cost?

Yes — parging is not just cosmetic. It helps protect exposed foundation walls from moisture penetration and freeze-thaw damage, which can lead to more expensive structural repairs if ignored.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does parging cost per square foot?
Typically between $5 and $15 per square foot depending on condition and finish type.

Is acrylic parging more expensive?
Yes, acrylic finishes cost more but offer better flexibility and moisture resistance.

Why do parging prices vary so much?
Surface condition, prep work, and repair needs significantly affect total cost.

Can I just patch damaged parging?
Small areas can be patched, but widespread damage often requires full resurfacing for durability.
