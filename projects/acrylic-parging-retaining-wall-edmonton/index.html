Acrylic Finish Application on Concrete Retaining Wall

This project involved a specialized acrylic parging application for a residential retaining wall in Edmonton. While the home featured a full stucco system, the owner required a durable, matching finish for the concrete landscaping elements to ensure a cohesive look and long-term protection against moisture.

Project Specifications

Unlike standard cement parging, an acrylic finish offers superior flexibility and color consistency. For this Edmonton property, we utilized a high-grade acrylic topcoat to prevent the hairline cracking often seen in traditional masonry.

Why Use Acrylic for Retaining Walls?

Retaining walls are under constant pressure from soil moisture and freeze-thaw cycles. We recommended this specific system for three reasons:

  • Moisture Resistance: Acrylic creates a breathable but water-resistant barrier.
  • Aesthetic Matching: We perfectly matched the parging to the home's main stucco color.
  • Durability: Highly resistant to the salt and chemicals used in Edmonton winters.

Restoring vs. Resurfacing

If your current foundation looks like this wall before our intervention, you might be wondering about the cost of parging in Edmonton. We provide options ranging from localized parging repairs to full acrylic resurfacing like shown in this project.

The AxisLayer Execution

Our team properly prepped the raw concrete surface with professional-grade bonding agents. This is a critical step often skipped by general contractors. For more information on our full suite of foundation protections, visit our parging services page.

Project FAQ

Is acrylic parging more expensive than standard cement?
Yes, acrylic materials have a higher price point but offer significantly longer lifespans and better crack resistance in the Alberta climate.

How long did this retaining wall take?
This specific project was completed in 2 days, allowing for proper drying time between the base coat and the final acrylic texture.

Can you match the parging color to my existing stucco?
Absolutely. Since acrylic finishes come in a wide variety of tints, we can precisely match the color of your home's stucco to ensure the retaining wall or foundation looks like a seamless part of the original design.
