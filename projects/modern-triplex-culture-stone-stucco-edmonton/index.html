Mixed Material Facade: Charcoal Stone & Acrylic Stucco

This modern triplex project in Edmonton highlights the seamless integration of charcoal stacked stone veneer and high-performance acrylic stucco. The goal was to create a bold, contemporary aesthetic that provides maximum durability for a multi-unit residential investment.

Project Specifications

For this build, we utilized a combination of texture and tone to define the architectural lines. The culture stone installation was used as a vertical feature to break up the stucco elevations, providing a "heavy" visual base that grounds the building's design.

Why Mix Culture Stone with Stucco?

Modern infill projects in Edmonton rely on material variety to satisfy zoning aesthetics and buyer demand. We recommended this combination for three specific reasons:

  • Architectural Depth: The charcoal stacked stone adds a 3D texture that smooth stucco cannot achieve alone.
  • Material Transition: We installed specialized drainage flashing at every point where the stone meets the stucco.
  • Curb Appeal: The dark charcoal finish provides a high-contrast look that is currently trending in the Edmonton market.

The AxisLayer Execution

On a multi-unit project, consistency is key. We ensured that the stone patterns and mortar joints were identical across all three units. Project FAQ

Is culture stone as durable as real stone in Edmonton?
Yes. Modern manufactured culture stone is designed to withstand Alberta’s freeze-thaw cycles without the excessive weight or cost of full-bed stone.

How do you prevent water from getting behind the stone?
We install a secondary moisture barrier and professional flashing at all transition points where the stone meets the stucco or the foundation parging.

Can I add this stone to an existing stucco house?
Absolutely. We can perform parging repairs and simultaneously install stone veneers over existing concrete or properly prepared wood-frame walls.
