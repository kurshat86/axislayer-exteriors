Why Parging Cracks — And What It Means for Your Foundation

Seeing cracks in your foundation parging is common in Edmonton, especially after winter. While small cracks can be cosmetic, larger or spreading cracks may allow moisture in and lead to further damage over time.

What Causes Parging to Crack?

The most common cause of cracked parging in Edmonton is the freeze-thaw cycle. Moisture enters small surface gaps, freezes during winter, and expands — causing the parging layer to crack and separate from the foundation.

Other causes include poor surface preparation, aging materials, and natural settling of the home.

When Cracked Parging Becomes a Problem

  • Cracks continue to grow or spread
  • Parging begins to crumble or fall off
  • Moisture stains appear on the surface
  • Exposed concrete is visible underneath

In these cases, it’s best to address the issue early before more extensive repairs are needed.

Can You Ignore Cracked Parging?

Small hairline cracks may not require immediate repair, but ignoring larger cracks can allow moisture to penetrate the surface. Over time, this can accelerate deterioration and lead to more costly repairs.

How Parging Repair Works

Repairing cracked parging typically involves removing loose or damaged areas, preparing the surface, and applying a new parging coat to restore both protection and appearance.

If you’re dealing with visible cracks or deteriorating surfaces, professional foundation parging in Edmonton can restore the surface and help protect your home long-term.

Why This Happens More in Edmonton

Edmonton’s climate creates repeated freeze-thaw cycles throughout the year. This constant expansion and contraction puts stress on exterior coatings like parging, making cracking one of the most common issues homeowners experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is cracked parging serious?
Small cracks may be cosmetic, but larger cracks can allow moisture in and should be repaired.

Does cracked parging need to be replaced?
Not always. Minor damage can be repaired, while more severe deterioration may require full resurfacing.
