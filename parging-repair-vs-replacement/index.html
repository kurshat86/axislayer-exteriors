Parging Repair vs. Full Replacement

Choosing the Right Path for Your Foundation

When your foundation exterior looks worn, you’re faced with a choice: do you patch the visible cracks or strip it back for a full redo? In Edmonton's climate, making the wrong choice can lead to "throwing good money after bad." Here is how to decide based on cost, longevity, and results.

Option 1: Parging Repair (The "Patch")

Repairing parging involves cleaning out specific cracks or small hollow areas and filling them with new material.

When to choose it:

  • The rest of the parging is solid and doesn't sound "hollow" when tapped.
  • You have isolated damage (e.g., from a shovel hit or a single settling crack).
  • You are on a tight budget and need immediate moisture protection.

For localized fixes, our parging repair service specializes in blending new material with your existing foundation for a clean finish.

Option 2: Full Replacement (The "Redo")

A full replacement involves removing the old parging entirely, grinding the concrete surface to ensure a perfect bond, and applying a fresh, uniform layer across the entire foundation.

When to choose it:

  • Widespread Delamination: If parging is peeling or falling off in multiple areas.
  • Longevity: You want a solution that will last 15+ years without maintenance.
  • Curb Appeal: You want the "new home" look with a perfectly smooth, uniform finish.

If your foundation is showing severe signs of wear, you can use our parging assessment guide to help determine if your coating has reached the end of its useful life.

Cost vs. Value in Edmonton

While a full replacement costs more upfront, it often offers better value. In Edmonton, freeze-thaw cycles find the "weak spots" where old parging meets new patches. If your original parging is already failing, patching one area often leads to another area failing the following year.

Investing in professional foundation parging in Edmonton eliminates the cycle of yearly repairs and provides a complete weather seal for your home.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does parging repair cost compared to replacement?
Repairing a few cracks is significantly cheaper upfront. However, if the parging is failing globally, a full replacement is more cost-effective over 10 years because it prevents recurring repair costs. You can see detailed parging price comparisons for both small and large projects in our latest guide.
Can you see the difference after a parging patch?
Even with expert color matching, new parging rarely blends perfectly with weathered parging. A full replacement ensures a 100% uniform finish and maximum curb appeal.
How long does a parging replacement last in Edmonton?
A professionally applied, fully bonded parging system in Edmonton can last 15-20 years or more, whereas patches on an aging surface may only last 2-5 years.

The Verdict

If your parging is over 10 years old and showing signs of widespread cracking or peeling, replacement is the smarter investment. If your home is newer and the damage is strictly local, a professional repair is likely all you need.
