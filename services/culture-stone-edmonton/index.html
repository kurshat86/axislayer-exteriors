What Is Culture Stone?

Culture stone (also called stone veneer) is a manufactured cladding system designed to replicate natural stone without full masonry construction. It is lightweight, versatile, and commonly used in residential design for both interior and exterior accent applications.

Where Culture Stone Is Used

Interior Applications

  • Fireplace Feature Walls: High-impact focal point upgrades for living spaces.
  • Interior Accent Walls: Modern architectural design features for homes and condos.

Exterior Applications

  • Entry Accents: Front entrances, columns, and porch walls.
  • Architectural Features: Select façade sections for curb appeal improvement.

Note: installations are used for accent areas only, not full exterior cladding systems.

Stone Styles We Install

  • Stacked Stone – Clean linear finish used for modern fireplaces and feature walls.
  • Ledgestone – Natural textured stone with depth and variation for architectural features.
  • River Rock – Rounded traditional stone for natural aesthetic designs.

Built for Edmonton Climate

All installations are engineered for Alberta’s freeze-thaw cycles. Proper moisture barriers, bonding systems, and substrate preparation are used to prevent cracking, detachment, and water intrusion over time.

Installation Process

  1. Surface Preparation: Substrate inspection and moisture barrier installation.
  2. Design Layout: Pattern selection and stone placement planning.
  3. Installation: High-bond mortar application and secure stone setting.
  4. Finishing: Joint detailing and weather-resistant sealing for durability.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is culture stone the same as stone veneer?

Yes. Culture stone is a common term for manufactured stone veneer used in residential and commercial applications.

Can culture stone be used outside in Edmonton weather?

Yes. When properly installed with moisture barriers and correct adhesives, it is designed to withstand freeze-thaw cycles.

How long does installation take?

Most residential feature walls or fireplace installations take 1–3 days depending on size and surface preparation.

Can it be installed over existing stucco or siding?

In many cases yes, but it depends on substrate condition. A proper assessment is required before installation.

What areas do you service?
